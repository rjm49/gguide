ee -> e

Verbs with a 'long e' (an 'ee' sound) often take a 'short e' in the SIMPLE PAST.

These include :

  • keep -> kept
  • sleep -> slept
  • bleed -> bled
  • lead(say 'leed') -> led

Be careful! There are exceptions:

  • need is regular -> needed
  • mean -> meant (say 'ment')
